The Basics

The goal of this game is to guess the drawing that is made by the artist on the canvas. This game is played in rounds. For each round, an artist will be selected to draw a given word. Everyone else has to guess what that word is under the "Game" text box. Each round lasts three minutes, but the timer will drop to 20 seconds (or 5 seconds if there are two players) as soon as the first correct guess is made. There are no penalties for wrong guesses, so guess anything that comes to your mind!

The first person to correctly guess the word gets 10 points, second person gets nine, third gets eight, and so on.

As the artist, you are given several tools that allow you to draw. You must draw the word given to you (otherwise nobody can guess it correctly!) You are not allowed to draw any letters. You can select different colors with the paintbrush ("üñåÔ∏è") button, erase with the eraser ("▰") button, and undo your previous stroke with the "↩" button. You can also adjust the width of your strokes with the slider next to the undo button. The "clear" button will completely wipe out any drawing you have made. The "skip" button will end the round early and select a new artist. You can give hints to what the word is with the "hint" button.

Lastly, you can chat and socialize with other players under the "Social" text box. Guesses towards the drawing are NOT checked in this box.

Got it? Good! You can click anywhere on this page to make this box disappear. Thank you for playing. I hope you have fun! - Neil Dunn

WARNING

This is still under development and may crash and burn unexpectedly. There is a known bug where a new artist does not get selected when a non-artist leaves the game. If this happens, just press "skip". Although I am actively searching for bugs and making several test cases, I cannot guarantee this will run smoothly 100% of the time. If you think something happened that was not supposed to, please let me know.

About this game

This is a simple remake of an older flash game known as iSketch. I had the idea of making this as a final project for one of my courses at the University of Kansas, but instead I decided to make it during my free time.
